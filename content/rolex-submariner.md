The Rolex Submariner, specifically the references 116610LN, 126610LN, and 124060, is one of the most iconic and sought-after luxury watches in the world. However, its popularity has also made it a prime target for counterfeiters. With the market flooded with fakes, distinguishing a genuine piece from a replica can be daunting. The stakes are high, as a counterfeit watch can cost thousands, both financially and in terms of reputation. This guide provides detailed insights into authenticating these specific Submariner models, focusing on the nuances that separate the real from the fake.

## Dial Printing and Text

The dial of a genuine Rolex Submariner should exhibit flawless printing. The text should be crisp and clear, with no bleeding or smudging. On the 116610LN and 126610LN, the 'Submariner' text is printed in white, while the 124060 features a simple, clean design without a date window. The 'Oyster Perpetual Date' or 'Oyster Perpetual' text should align perfectly, with consistent spacing and font size. Check the 'Swiss Made' inscription at the 6 o'clock position, which should be precisely printed and centred.

## The Cyclops Magnification

The 116610LN and 126610LN models feature a date window with a cyclops lens that magnifies the date by 2.5 times. Counterfeit models often fail to achieve this level of magnification, resulting in a smaller or distorted date display. The cyclops should be perfectly aligned over the date window, and the magnification should be clear and undistorted.

## Caseback and Engravings

Rolex Submariners have a solid caseback with no engravings, a feature often overlooked by counterfeiters who sometimes add unnecessary markings. The caseback should be smooth and crafted from high-quality stainless steel. If you encounter a Submariner with an engraved caseback, it is likely a fake.

## Movement and Caliber

The 116610LN is powered by the Rolex Calibre 3135, while the 126610LN and 124060 house the Calibre 3235 and 3230 respectively. These movements are known for their precision and reliability. Genuine Rolex movements are beautifully finished, with no rough edges or unfinished parts. Counterfeit movements often lack the finesse and precision of genuine Rolex calibres.

## Lume Colour and Application

Rolex Submariners use Chromalight luminescent material, which emits a blue glow in low-light conditions. The lume should be evenly applied and glow consistently. Counterfeit models might use inferior lume that appears greenish or fades quickly. Check the brightness and duration of the lume to ensure authenticity.

## Bezel Insert and Action

The bezel insert on these Submariner models is made from Cerachrom, a highly durable ceramic material. It should have a glossy finish and the numerals should be filled with platinum. The bezel action should be smooth and precise, with 120 distinct clicks per rotation. A fake might have a bezel that feels loose or has fewer clicks.

## Bracelet End-Links and Clasp

The Submariner's Oyster bracelet should fit seamlessly with the case, with no gaps or misalignment. The end-links should be solid and the clasp should feature the Rolex Glidelock system, allowing for easy micro-adjustments. Check for the correct bracelet code, such as '97200' for the 116610LN, engraved on the inner clasp.

## Weight and Sound

A genuine Rolex Submariner has a substantial weight due to its high-quality materials. If the watch feels unusually light, it could be a fake. Additionally, listen for the sound of the movement; a genuine Rolex should have a smooth, almost silent tick, unlike the louder ticking of a counterfeit.

## Red Flags at a Glance

- Misaligned cyclops lens
- Engraved caseback
- Incorrect lume colour
- Loose bezel action
- Lightweight feel
- Inconsistent dial printing
- Missing Glidelock clasp
- Incorrect bracelet code
- Distorted date magnification
- Noisy movement

## FAQ

The Rolex Submariner 116610LN is powered by the Calibre 3135 movement.

### How can I verify the authenticity of the lume on a Submariner?

Genuine Rolex Submariners use Chromalight, which emits a blue glow. Check for even application and consistent brightness.

### What should the bezel action feel like on a real Submariner?

The bezel should rotate with 120 distinct clicks, offering a smooth and precise action.

### Are there any engravings on the caseback of a genuine Submariner?

No, a genuine Rolex Submariner has a solid, unengraved caseback.

### What bracelet code should be on a Submariner 116610LN?

The correct bracelet code for a Submariner 116610LN is '97200', found on the inner clasp.

With the prevalence of high-quality counterfeits, a thorough examination is essential to ensure the authenticity of your investment.
