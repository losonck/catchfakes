The Rolex Sea-Dweller, with its robust build and iconic design, is a favourite among diving enthusiasts and collectors alike. However, its popularity has also made it a prime target for counterfeiters. The Sea-Dweller models 126600, 116600, and 16600 are particularly susceptible to imitation due to their high demand and premium price point. With fakes becoming increasingly sophisticated, it's crucial for buyers to know how to authenticate these specific references. The stakes are high—purchasing a counterfeit not only results in financial loss but also diminishes the prestige associated with owning a genuine Rolex. This guide will arm you with the knowledge needed to identify authentic Sea-Dweller watches and avoid the pitfalls of the counterfeit market.

## The Cyclops Magnification

The Rolex Sea-Dweller 126600 is the first in the series to feature the Cyclops lens over the date. Genuine models have a 2.5x magnification, which is perfectly centred and clear. Counterfeit versions often have incorrect magnification levels, usually less than 2.5x, and the lens may be slightly off-centre or poorly aligned with the date window.

## Caseback Engravings

The caseback of the Sea-Dweller 16600 and 116600 is solid and devoid of any engravings, maintaining Rolex's tradition of understated elegance. However, the 126600 features the 'Rolex Oyster Original Gas Escape Valve' and 'Ring Lock System' engravings. These engravings should be crisp, evenly spaced, and free from any spelling errors. Counterfeits often have poorly executed engravings that are either too shallow or inconsistent in font and spacing.

## Bracelet End-Links and Clasp

Authentic Sea-Dweller models use the Oyster bracelet with solid end-links. The 126600 features the Oysterlock clasp with the Glidelock extension system, allowing for micro-adjustments. The bracelet code for the 126600 is 97200. The 116600 and 16600 use the Oysterlock clasp with the Fliplock extension, and their bracelet codes are 93160 and 93160A, respectively. Counterfeit bracelets often feel lighter, have rough edges, and the clasp mechanism may not function smoothly.

## Movement Decoration

The Sea-Dweller 126600 houses the calibre 3235, while the 116600 and 16600 are equipped with the calibre 3135. These movements are known for their precision and reliability. Authentic movements feature high-quality decoration, including the Rolex Parachrom hairspring and Perpetual rotor. Counterfeit movements may lack these details and often use generic, undecorated movements that do not match the performance or appearance of genuine Rolex calibres.

## Bezel Insert and Action

The bezel on the Sea-Dweller 126600 is made of Cerachrom ceramic, known for its scratch resistance and deep black colour. The 116600 also features a ceramic bezel, while the 16600 uses an aluminium insert. Genuine bezels have precise, firm clicks with no play, and the markings are filled with platinum or white gold. Counterfeit bezels may have a dull finish, uneven markings, and a loose or inconsistent action when rotated.

## Lume Colour and Application

Rolex Sea-Dwellers use Chromalight lume, which emits a blue glow in the dark. The application is even and consistent across the dial and hands. Counterfeit models often use inferior lume that may appear greenish or unevenly applied, leading to patchy illumination.

## Weight and Sound

A genuine Sea-Dweller feels substantial due to its use of high-quality materials. The 126600, in particular, is heavier due to its larger size and additional features. A counterfeit may feel lighter and less solid. Additionally, the sound of the movement's rotor in a genuine Rolex is almost silent, whereas fakes may produce a noticeable noise when the watch is moved.

## Red Flags at a Glance

- Incorrect Cyclops magnification
- Poorly executed caseback engravings
- Rough bracelet edges
- Generic movement appearance
- Dull bezel finish
- Greenish or uneven lume
- Lightweight feel
- Noisy rotor movement

## FAQ

### What movement does the Sea-Dweller 126600 use?

The Sea-Dweller 126600 is equipped with the Rolex calibre 3235 movement.

### Does the Sea-Dweller 16600 have a ceramic bezel?

No, the Sea-Dweller 16600 features an aluminium bezel insert, not ceramic.

### How can I verify the bracelet code on a Sea-Dweller?

Check the inside of the clasp for the bracelet code: 97200 for 126600, 93160 for 116600, and 93160A for 16600.

### What is the lume colour on a genuine Sea-Dweller?

Authentic Sea-Dwellers use Chromalight lume, which emits a blue glow.

### Can the Sea-Dweller 116600's bezel rotate in both directions?

No, the bezel on the Sea-Dweller 116600 is unidirectional, rotating only counterclockwise.
