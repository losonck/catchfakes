The Omega Seamaster Aqua Terra 220.10.41.21.03.001 is a highly sought-after timepiece, known for its elegant design and robust functionality. ## The Dial Printing

The Omega Seamaster Aqua Terra 220.10.41.21.03.001 features a distinctive blue dial with a teak pattern that runs vertically. Authentic dials have crisp, precise printing, especially noticeable in the 'Omega' logo and the 'Co-Axial Master Chronometer' text. Counterfeits often have fuzzy or misaligned text. The minute markers should be uniformly spaced and sharply printed.

## Crown Action and Logo

The crown of the Aqua Terra should operate smoothly with a precise click when setting the time. It bears the Omega logo, which should be deeply engraved and perfectly centred. Counterfeit crowns may feel loose or have a poorly executed logo, often appearing shallow or off-centre.

## Caseback Engravings

The caseback of the 220.10.41.21.03.001 is transparent, showcasing the Omega Calibre 8900 movement. Authentic models have a sapphire crystal caseback with anti-reflective treatment, and the engravings should be sharp, detailing the model number and other specifications. Counterfeits might have poorly executed engravings or incorrect information.

## Movement Decoration

The Omega Calibre 8900 movement is a hallmark of the Aqua Terra, featuring rhodium plating and Geneva waves in arabesque. The movement should be visible through the transparent caseback, with a meticulous finish. Fake watches often have a generic movement lacking the distinctive decoration and precision of the genuine article.

## Lume Colour and Quality

The lume on the Aqua Terra 220.10.41.21.03.001 is Super-LumiNova, glowing blue in the dark. Authentic watches have a consistent and bright lume application, whereas fakes may have uneven or dim lume that fades quickly.

## Bracelet End-Links and Clasp

This model typically comes with a stainless steel bracelet, reference 020STZ004250. The end-links should fit seamlessly with the case, without gaps or misalignment. The clasp should be engraved with the Omega logo and feel robust, with a secure locking mechanism. Counterfeit bracelets often have poor fit and finish, with loose end-links and flimsy clasps.

## Weight and Sound

An authentic Omega Seamaster Aqua Terra has a substantial weight due to its high-quality materials. It should feel solid on the wrist. When shaken gently, the movement should not produce any rattling sounds. Fakes are often lighter and may have a noticeable rattle, indicating inferior construction.

## Red Flags at a Glance
