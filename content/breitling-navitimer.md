The Breitling Navitimer AB0127211B1A1 is a revered timepiece among collectors and aviation enthusiasts alike. Known for its intricate chronograph functions and iconic design, it is a model that commands respect and a significant financial investment. However, with its prestige comes the risk of encountering counterfeits. The counterfeit market for the Navitimer is particularly robust, making it crucial for potential buyers to be equipped with the knowledge to discern genuine pieces from fakes. This guide will walk you through the critical details that experts scrutinise to ensure authenticity.

## The Dial Printing

The Breitling Navitimer AB0127211B1A1 features a meticulously detailed dial that should exhibit crisp and clear printing. The sub-dials are perfectly aligned, and the numerals are sharp with no bleeding or smudging. The logo should be embossed and centred correctly, with the winged 'B' emblem precisely executed. Any signs of misalignment or poor-quality printing are immediate red flags.

## Movement and Calibre

This Navitimer houses the Breitling Calibre 01 movement, known for its robustness and precision. A genuine movement will have a smooth, sweeping second hand, and when opened, the movement should display high-quality finishing with the Breitling logo engraved on the rotor. Counterfeit movements often lack this level of detail and may have jerky second-hand movements.

## Rehaut Engraving

The rehaut, or the inner bezel, of the Navitimer AB0127211B1A1 should have precise engravings. Look for the serial number and 'Breitling' text engraved with laser precision. These engravings should be clear and deep, not shallow or blurred, which are common traits of counterfeit models.

## Caseback Details

The caseback of the Navitimer AB0127211B1A1 is solid and should feature a detailed engraving of the Breitling logo, model number, and other specifications. The engraving should be sharp and consistent, with no spelling errors or uneven font sizes. Counterfeits often have poorly executed engravings that appear shallow or inconsistent.

## Bracelet and Clasp

The bracelet of this model should be a stainless steel Navitimer bracelet with the code 447A. The links should fit seamlessly with no gaps, and the clasp should close securely with a satisfying click. The Breitling logo on the clasp should be deeply engraved, not printed or shallowly etched. Counterfeit bracelets often have loose links and inferior clasp mechanisms.

## Bezel and Slide Rule

The Navitimer's distinctive bi-directional bezel with a slide rule should rotate smoothly without any resistance. The bezel insert should be made from high-quality materials with clear, legible markings. Counterfeit models may have stiff or overly loose bezels and poorly printed slide rule scales.

## Lume Quality

The lume on the Navitimer AB0127211B1A1 should glow brightly and evenly in the dark. Genuine models use high-quality luminous material that retains brightness for extended periods. In contrast, counterfeit watches often use inferior lume that fades quickly and appears uneven.

## Red Flags at a Glance

- Blurry or misaligned dial printing
- Jerky second-hand movement
- Shallow rehaut engravings
- Inconsistent caseback engravings
- Loose bracelet links
- Stiff or loose bezel rotation
- Uneven or dim lume
