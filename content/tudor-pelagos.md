The Tudor Pelagos, particularly the 25600TN, 25600TB, and 25407N models, is a highly sought-after dive watch known for its robust design and professional-grade features. However, with its popularity comes the inevitable rise of counterfeits. These replicas can be deceivingly convincing, making it crucial for potential buyers to be well-versed in authentication. The financial stakes are high, as genuine models can command significant prices in the secondary market, while a counterfeit might leave you with a worthless imitation. The counterfeit market for the Tudor Pelagos is sophisticated, with fakes often replicating even the most minute details. This guide will arm you with the knowledge needed to discern genuine from fake, ensuring your investment is sound.

## The Dial Printing

Authentic Tudor Pelagos dials are impeccably printed. The text should be sharp and clear, with no bleeding or smudging. Pay close attention to the 'PELAGOS' text and the depth rating; these should be perfectly aligned and evenly spaced. The lume plots on genuine models are applied with precision, appearing slightly raised and perfectly circular. Counterfeits often have uneven lume application and fuzzy text.

## Crown Action and Engravings

The crown of a genuine Tudor Pelagos should operate smoothly, with a precise screw-down action. The Tudor logo on the crown should be deeply engraved and centred. Fake models often have poorly executed engravings that are shallow or off-centre, and the crown action may feel gritty or loose.

## Rehaut Engraving

The rehaut, or the inner flange of the watch, should have a subtle but precise engraving of the Tudor name. This engraving is often overlooked by counterfeiters or poorly executed. On a genuine Pelagos, the engraving is crisp and perfectly aligned with the dial markers.

## Caseback and Movement

The caseback of the Tudor Pelagos is solid and features the Tudor shield logo and reference number. Inside, the movement should be the Tudor Calibre MT5612 for the 25600TN and 25600TB, or MT5402 for the 25407N. These movements are decorated with a distinctive rotor and bear the Tudor name. Counterfeits may use generic movements that lack this decoration and precision.

## Lume Colour and Quality

The lume on a genuine Tudor Pelagos glows a bright blue, thanks to its Swiss Super-LumiNova application. It should be consistent across all markers and hands. Fakes often use inferior lume that may glow greenish or fade quickly.

## Bezel Insert Material

The bezel insert on a real Tudor Pelagos is made from ceramic, which is highly scratch-resistant and has a matte finish. The numerals and markers should be engraved and filled with lume. Fake models might use aluminium inserts, which are more prone to scratches and have painted numerals.

## Bracelet End-Links and Clasp

The bracelet of a genuine Tudor Pelagos should have a solid and robust feel. The end-links should fit perfectly against the case with no gaps. The clasp is equipped with a unique spring-loaded extension system and should bear the Tudor logo. Counterfeits often have poorly fitting end-links and lack the precise extension mechanism.

## Weight and Sound

A genuine Tudor Pelagos has a substantial weight due to its titanium construction, which is lighter than steel but still solid. When handling the watch, it should feel balanced and not overly light. The rotor movement inside should be smooth and quiet, unlike the noisy or rattling sound often found in fakes.

## Red Flags at a Glance

- Fuzzy or misaligned dial text
- Gritty crown action
- Shallow or off-centre crown engraving
- Poorly executed rehaut engraving
- Greenish or inconsistent lume
- Aluminium bezel insert instead of ceramic
- Loose or poorly fitting bracelet end-links
- Noisy movement

## FAQ

### What movement should a Tudor Pelagos have?

The Tudor Pelagos 25600TN and 25600TB should have the Calibre MT5612, while the 25407N should have the MT5402.

### How can I verify the lume quality on a Tudor Pelagos?

A genuine Tudor Pelagos will have a bright blue lume, applied evenly across all markers and hands.

### What material is the bezel insert of a Tudor Pelagos?

The bezel insert on a genuine Tudor Pelagos is made from ceramic, which is scratch-resistant and has a matte finish.

## Final Word

Authenticating a Tudor Pelagos requires a keen eye for detail and an understanding of the specific features that define this iconic watch. While this guide provides a comprehensive overview, consider seeking a second opinion from a professional watchmaker or authentication service to ensure your purchase is genuine. Investing in a Tudor Pelagos should be a rewarding experience, free from the pitfalls of counterfeits.
