The Vacheron Constantin Overseas 4500V/110A-B126 is a masterpiece of luxury watchmaking, renowned for its elegant design and technical prowess. However, its prestige and demand have also made it a prime target for counterfeiters. Falling victim to a fake can result in a significant financial loss, as these watches retail for tens of thousands of pounds. The counterfeit market for the Overseas model is unfortunately thriving, with replicas becoming increasingly sophisticated. This guide will arm you with the knowledge to discern an authentic Vacheron Constantin Overseas 4500V from a fake, focusing on the nuances that even skilled counterfeiters often overlook.

## Dial Printing and Details

The dial of the Vacheron Constantin Overseas 4500V/110A-B126 is a marvel of precision. Authentic models feature a finely detailed sunburst blue dial with a subtle gradient effect. The 'Vacheron Constantin' text and Maltese cross logo should be crisply printed, with no bleeding or smudging. The date window at 3 o'clock is perfectly aligned, and the date disc matches the dial colour seamlessly. Counterfeit models often have uneven printing and misaligned date windows.

## Movement Caliber 5100

The heart of the 4500V is the in-house Caliber 5100, visible through the sapphire caseback. This movement is beautifully decorated with Geneva stripes and bears the Hallmark of Geneva. The rotor is made of 22K gold, featuring a Maltese cross motif. Fakes may use generic movements with less decoration and no hallmark. The movement should operate smoothly, with no irregular ticking sounds.

## Bezel and Case Material

The bezel of the 4500V is made from stainless steel, featuring a distinctive hexagonal shape with a polished finish. The case is also stainless steel, with a mix of brushed and polished surfaces. Counterfeit models might use inferior materials, leading to a duller appearance and less precise finishing. Check for sharp edges and inconsistent polishing as signs of a fake.

## Bracelet and Clasp

The bracelet of the 4500V is a seamless integration of form and function, crafted from stainless steel with a satin-brushed finish. The bracelet code is engraved on the inside of the clasp, which should operate smoothly and securely. The clasp features a double security system, and the fit between the bracelet and the case should be perfect, with no gaps or play. Fakes often have poorly fitting bracelets and subpar clasp mechanisms.

## Lume Colour and Quality

The lume on the 4500V is applied to the hands and hour markers, glowing a bright and consistent blue in the dark. Authentic lume should last for several hours after exposure to light. Counterfeit models may have uneven lume application or a greenish tint, and the glow may fade quickly.

## Weight and Balance

An authentic Vacheron Constantin Overseas 4500V has a substantial weight due to its high-quality materials and robust construction. It should feel balanced on the wrist, without any top-heaviness. Counterfeits often use lighter materials, resulting in a watch that feels insubstantial or unbalanced.

## Red Flags at a Glance

- Misaligned date window
- Uneven dial printing
- No Hallmark of Geneva on movement
- Poorly fitting bracelet
- Greenish or uneven lume
- Lightweight feel

## FAQ

### How can I verify the movement of a Vacheron Constantin Overseas?

Check for the Caliber 5100 with Geneva stripes and the Hallmark of Geneva. The rotor should be 22K gold with a Maltese cross.

### What should the lume look like on a genuine 4500V?

The lume should glow a bright blue consistently and last for several hours after light exposure.

### Are there specific bracelet codes for the 4500V?

Yes, the bracelet code is engraved inside the clasp and should match the high-quality finish of the bracelet.

### What materials are used in the bezel of the 4500V?

The bezel is crafted from stainless steel with a polished finish, featuring a unique hexagonal shape.

### How can I spot a fake Vacheron Constantin Overseas quickly?

Look for misaligned date windows, uneven printing, and check the weight and lume quality for quick red flags.
