The Rolex Datejust, particularly the 126234 and 116234 references, represents a pinnacle of timeless elegance and precision. However, the popularity of these models has made them prime targets for counterfeiters. Falling victim to a fake can be a costly mistake, with counterfeit models often being sold for thousands of pounds. Understanding the nuances that distinguish a genuine Rolex Datejust from a counterfeit is crucial for any potential buyer. This guide provides an in-depth analysis of the key features to scrutinise when authenticating these specific references.

## The Cyclops Magnification

One of the most distinctive features of a Rolex Datejust is the Cyclops lens over the date window. Genuine models of the 126234 and 116234 should exhibit a 2.5x magnification of the date. Counterfeit models often fail to achieve this level of magnification, resulting in a date that appears smaller and less readable. Examine the clarity and size of the date through the Cyclops; if it appears distorted or under-magnified, this is a red flag.

## Caseback Engravings

Rolex Datejust models, including the 126234 and 116234, feature a smooth, polished caseback with no engravings. Counterfeit versions may mistakenly include engravings or markings on the caseback, which is a clear indicator of a fake. Always check for the absence of any unnecessary engravings on the caseback.

## Movement and Calibre

The Rolex Datejust 126234 is powered by the Calibre 3235, while the 116234 houses the Calibre 3135. These movements are known for their precision and reliability. A genuine movement will feature intricate decorations and the signature Rolex Parachrom hairspring. Counterfeit movements may lack these details and often exhibit inferior craftsmanship. If possible, have the movement inspected by a professional to ensure authenticity.

## Bracelet End-Links and Clasp

The 126234 and 116234 models typically come with the Jubilee bracelet, featuring solid end-links that fit seamlessly against the case. The clasp should have a crisp, laser-etched Rolex coronet. Counterfeit bracelets may have loose end-links and poorly executed engravings on the clasp. Additionally, genuine Rolex bracelets are marked with a specific code inside the clasp, corresponding to the production year.

## Bezel Material and Finish

The bezel of the 126234 and 116234 is crafted from 18k white gold, giving it a distinct lustre and weight. Counterfeit bezels may use inferior materials that lack the same sheen and durability. The fluted design should be sharp and precise, with each groove perfectly aligned. Any signs of unevenness or poor finishing are indicative of a fake.

## Rehaut Engraving

The rehaut, or the inner flange, of genuine Rolex Datejust models is engraved with the serial number and the Rolex name. The engraving should be sharp and perfectly aligned with the minute markers. Counterfeit models may have poorly executed engravings that are misaligned or lack depth. Inspect the rehaut closely for these details.

## Dial Printing and Lume

The dial of a genuine Datejust should have immaculate printing, with each letter and marker perfectly aligned. The lume on the hands and hour markers should glow brightly and consistently when exposed to light. Counterfeit dials may have smudged or uneven printing, and the lume may be dull or inconsistent.

## Red Flags at a Glance

- Cyclops lens does not magnify 2.5x
- Engravings on the caseback
- Inferior movement craftsmanship
- Loose bracelet end-links
- Poorly executed clasp engravings
- Bezel lacks lustre and precision
- Misaligned rehaut engravings
- Smudged dial printing
- Inconsistent lume glow
