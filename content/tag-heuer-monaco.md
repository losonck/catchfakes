## Dial Printing and Sub-Dials

The dial of the TAG Heuer Monaco CBL2113.FC6177 should exhibit crisp and precise printing, particularly on the sub-dials. The sub-dials should be perfectly aligned and the printing should be sharp, with no bleeding or smudging. The Monaco logo and TAG Heuer shield should be centred and proportionate. The colour of the dial should be a deep blue, consistent throughout, without any variations or fading.

## Crown and Pushers

The crown of the Monaco CBL2113.FC6177 is located at the 9 o'clock position, a unique feature of the Monaco series. It should screw down smoothly and feel robust. The pushers, located at 2 and 4 o'clock, should operate with a firm, precise action. Any looseness or sponginess is a red flag.

## Caseback Engravings

The caseback of this Monaco model should feature detailed engravings that include the TAG Heuer logo, the model number, and 'Swiss Made'. These engravings should be deep and precise, not shallow or uneven. The caseback should also have a sapphire crystal exhibition window showcasing the movement.

## Movement and Decoration

Inside the CBL2113.FC6177 is the Calibre Heuer 02 automatic movement. This movement should be visible through the exhibition caseback and should display high-quality finishing, including Côtes de Genève on the rotor and bridges. The movement should be clean, with no signs of corrosion or poor assembly.

## Lume Colour and Application

The lume on the hands and indices of the Monaco CBL2113.FC6177 should glow consistently in low light, with a blue hue. The application should be even, with no blotches or uneven patches. If the lume is green or inconsistently applied, it may indicate a counterfeit.

## Strap and Buckle

This model typically comes with a high-quality leather strap, coded FC6177. The stitching should be even and the leather should feel supple yet sturdy. The buckle should bear the TAG Heuer logo and operate smoothly. Any rough edges or poor finishing on the buckle are signs of a fake.

## Red Flags at a Glance

- Loose crown or pushers
- Shallow caseback engravings
- Incorrect movement decoration
- Green or uneven lume
- Poor strap stitching
