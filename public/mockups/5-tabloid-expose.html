EXPOSÉ CASE 001 HIGH RISK

The Submariner: six checks that catch nine of every ten fakes.

By The Catch Fakes Authentication Desk · Filed 09.05.2026 · 11 minute read

The Submariner is the most counterfeited Rolex in circulation. Modern super-clones from Guangzhou have closed the visual gap to the point that photographs no longer reveal them. These are the six checks that still betray them.

The Rolex Submariner is one of the most iconic luxury watches in the world. It is also, by some distance, the most counterfeited. With the rise of so-called "super-clones" from Guangzhou and Shenzhen, the visual gap between genuine and fake has narrowed dramatically — but a handful of details, examined together, will still tell you almost everything you need to know.

1 — Dial Printing

Examine the dial under 10× magnification. Genuine Rolex printing is crisp, with no bleeding at the edges of small text. The 'Submariner' wordmark and 'Swiss Made' inscription should be perfectly aligned, with consistent kerning. Counterfeits often show feathering or off-center placement — and almost always fail at the printing depth around the applied indices.

RED FLAGS — IMMEDIATE FAIL

  • Caseback engravings of any kind (Rolex stopped this in the early '90s)
  • Cyclops magnifying less than 2× — measurable by eye
  • Bezel that turns smoothly without the 120 distinct clicks
  • Lume that glows green rather than blue
  • Bracelet end-links that show daylight against the case

2 — Cyclops

The cyclops on a real Rolex magnifies the date by exactly 2.5×. Counterfeit lenses rarely exceed 1.8×. The date should sit cleanly centered with no warp at the edges — a real cyclops projects a clean rectangular magnification, a fake bends and warps.

3 — Caseback

Rolex Submariner casebacks are smooth, solid stainless steel. No engravings, no logos, no model text. If you see anything engraved on the caseback, it is counterfeit — full stop.
