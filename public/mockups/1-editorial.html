Authentication Guide · Rolex Submariner

Spotting a Fake Submariner: 116610LN, 126610LN, 124060

The Submariner is the most counterfeited Rolex in circulation. Here is exactly where modern fakes still betray themselves — even the convincing ones.

The Rolex Submariner is one of the most iconic and sought-after luxury watches in the world. Its popularity has also made it a prime target for counterfeiters. With the market flooded with fakes, distinguishing a genuine piece from a replica can be daunting — but a handful of details, examined together, will tell you almost everything you need to know.

Dial Printing and Text

The dial of a genuine Rolex Submariner exhibits flawless printing. Text is crisp, with no bleeding or smudging. On the 116610LN and 126610LN, the 'Submariner' text is printed in white; the 124060 wears a simpler, no-date layout. The 'Swiss Made' inscription at six o'clock should be precisely centered with consistent kerning.

A counterfeit dial almost always reveals itself under a 10× loupe. The applied indices, the depth of the lacquer, and the alignment of the minute track are diagnostic.

The Cyclops Magnification

The cyclops on a real Rolex magnifies the date by 2.5×. Counterfeit cyclopses rarely achieve this — the date appears smaller, distorted, or sits noticeably off-center. View the watch from a slight angle: a real cyclops projects a clean rectangular magnification; a fake bends and warps at the edges.
