The Submariner: six checks that catch nine of every ten fakes.

Modern counterfeit Submariners have closed the visual gap to the point where photographs no longer reveal them. Six in-hand checks remain diagnostic. If three or more flag, the watch is almost certainly fake.

The Rolex Submariner is one of the most iconic luxury watches in the world. Its global recognisability has made it the most counterfeited Rolex in circulation. With the rise of so-called "super-clones" from Guangzhou and Shenzhen, the visual gap between genuine and counterfeit has narrowed dramatically — but a handful of details, examined together, will still tell you almost everything you need to know.

Dial Printing & Text

The dial of a genuine Rolex Submariner exhibits flawless printing. Text is crisp, with no bleeding or smudging. On the 116610LN and 126610LN, the 'Submariner' wordmark is printed in white. The 124060 wears the simpler, no-date layout. The 'Swiss Made' inscription at six o'clock should be precisely centered, with consistent kerning.

A counterfeit dial almost always reveals itself under a 10× loupe. The applied indices, the depth of the lacquer, and the alignment of the minute track are diagnostic.

The Cyclops Magnification

The cyclops on a real Rolex magnifies the date by 2.5×. Counterfeit cyclopses rarely achieve this — the date appears smaller, distorted, or sits noticeably off-center. View the watch from a slight angle: a real cyclops projects a clean rectangular magnification; a fake bends and warps at the edges of the lens.
