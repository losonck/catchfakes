Authentication Guide · Rolex Submariner

Spot a fake Submariner in six checks.

The Submariner is the most counterfeited Rolex in circulation. Modern super-clones have closed the visual gap to the point where photographs no longer reveal them. These are the six checks that still betray them.

The Rolex Submariner is one of the most iconic luxury watches in the world. It is also the most counterfeited. With the rise of so-called "super-clones" from Guangzhou and Shenzhen, the visual gap between genuine and fake has narrowed dramatically — but a handful of details, examined together, will still tell you almost everything you need to know.

1. Dial Printing

Examine the dial under 10× magnification. Genuine Rolex printing is crisp, with no bleeding at the edges of small text. The 'Submariner' wordmark and 'Swiss Made' inscription should be perfectly aligned, with consistent kerning. Counterfeits often show feathering or off-center placement.

A counterfeit dial almost always reveals itself under a 10× loupe. The applied indices, the depth of the lacquer, and the alignment of the minute track are diagnostic.

2. Cyclops Magnification

The cyclops on a real Rolex magnifies the date by exactly 2.5×. Counterfeit lenses rarely exceed 1.8×. The date should sit cleanly centered with no warp at the edges of the lens.
